Duck is not a frequent guest on the tables of a modern person, especially in everyday life. The only exceptions are the "lucky ones" who have their own farm and ducks. But this does not mean that you cannot please yourself and your loved ones with this delicious, and most importantly, quite simple dish to prepare. Moreover, if you want to find a quality duck carcass, it’s easy. In different countries of the world, baked duck with oranges is popular and has its own flavors. This is achieved by using various spices and marinating methods. We will not delve into each of them, but simply consider the most popular and proven recipe. We also clarify that in cooking a lot depends on who exactly cooks. As practice shows, even having the same set of products.
Ingredients may vary depending on your wishes and preferences. Now the recipe for cooking duck with oranges. And the first is the preparation of the bird. If it is already gutted and cleaned of feathers - great. If not, then proceed to this "dirty work". Be sure to remove the tail, excess skin around the neck and excess fat, especially if it does not appeal to its appearance. It is also worth getting rid of the outermost bones on the wings, otherwise, during baking, they will turn into charred, bad-smelling coals, which will nullify all your efforts. Now we mix the ingredients prepared in advance for our marinade in a deep container and immerse the duck there. Lubricate it well on all sides and leave it in the refrigerator for at least a night, and even better for a day.
Don't forget to flip the duck occasionally. This will allow the meat to soak as evenly as possible. In order for the duck baked with oranges to be guaranteed to come out juicy, put it in a deep baking sheet with the breast up. Cut the oranges and put them in the carcass. We send it to the oven already preheated to the desired temperature. It's about 190 degrees. After 50-60 minutes, when the juice begins to stand out, do not forget to water the duck periodically. Do not rely on the recipe for the time of readiness of the dish. The exact cooking time depends on the size of the bird. On average, it takes 2-2.5 hours. After your dish is ready, you need to prepare a delicious glaze. Mix the ingredients and put on a slow fire. Let the resulting syrup boil to a thick syrup.
Having taken the bird out of the oven, it is necessary to transfer it to a beautiful deep dish, put the oranges that were baked in it around the carcass and pour over everything with the resulting syrup. Now you know how to cook duck with oranges, which means you have the opportunity to show your guests your culinary skills and give a festive atmosphere to any day. After all, it is the aroma of a duck with a light fragrance of citrus that evokes in our head associations with a holiday, fun, and gives a feeling of magic! By the way, fresh oranges and some other fruits will serve as a decoration for the dish. Unobtrusive sweetness combined with the juicy taste of meat will definitely appeal to even the most demanding gourmets.

For many, the issue of garnish is relevant, and as practice shows, some housewives, not wanting to be creative, serve the most banal mashed potatoes with such a luxurious duck. But a vegetable salad would be much more appropriate. For example, a tomato salad, for which in one salad bowl you need to mix all kinds of tomatoes that you only have, add chopped garlic and basil to them. As a dressing, you can use any vegetable oil to taste or mayonnaise - it's a matter of preference. Such a light salad will be a chic addition to a soft and juicy duck. Dishes will turn out not only satisfying, but also useful.
We put the duck in the form, add orange juice and water, based on a 1: 1 ratio. Close the duck tightly with foil and send it to the oven. First, bake the duck with oranges at a temperature of 180 degrees for 40 minutes. Then we take the duck out of the oven, remove the foil, grease the duck with the secreted juice, put the foil on the legs and send it back to the oven. We bake the duck with oranges for another 1.5 hours at a temperature of 200-220 degrees. Periodically water the duck with the secreted juice. We stuff the duck with two peeled and chopped oranges and a cinnamon stick. Fasten with toothpicks. We decorate the finished baked duck with sliced ​​​​orange, fresh herbs and pomegranate seeds. Bon appetit and Happy New Year!
